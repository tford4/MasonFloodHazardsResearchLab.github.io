Magothy Bay Coastal Flood and Erosion Forecast System powered by XBEACH


The Mason Flood Hazards Research Lab has been studying storm surge and waves attenuation in the marshes of Magothy Bay Natural Area Preserve for the last 3 years. Our group has extensively monitored the hydrodynamic regime in the marshes and documented several hurricanes and storms.

This model produces coastal flooding and marsh erosion for current conditions and up to 84 hours into the future.

The top panel displays the most up to date conditions in the marsh. Field based vegetation survey is used to represent the marsh resistance to flooding and wave attenuation.

The bottom panel represents a hypothetical set-up where the vegetation is artificially removed from the system. In this animation we demonstrate, in real-time and for the next 84 hours, the impact of nature-based defenses for coastal protection. This simulations shows what the current conditions would be in the area without the presence of the vegetation.

The Hs plot below shows the waves 75m from the beginning of the marsh, also shown as the red dot in the video.
